# What is an Automatic Water Level Controller?

An Automatic Water Level Controller is an electronic control system that monitors water levels in storage tanks and automatically controls the water pump.

It can:

- Start the motor when the tank is low
- Stop the motor when the tank is full
- Protect the motor from dry running (if supported)
- Reduce water wastage
- Save electricity

---

# How Does It Work?

The controller uses sensors or float switches to detect water levels.

Typical operation:

1. Water level drops below the minimum level.
2. The controller starts the pump.
3. Water fills the overhead tank.
4. When the tank reaches the maximum level, the controller turns the pump off.

This automation reduces the need for manual monitoring.

# Types of Water Level Controllers

## Float Switch Based

Suitable for homes and farms. Simple, reliable, and cost-effective.

## Sensor Based

Uses conductive probes to detect water levels. Common in domestic applications.

## Wireless Controllers

Ideal when wiring between tanks is difficult.

## Industrial Controllers

Designed for factories, apartments, and commercial water systems.

# Installation Guide

Before installation:

- Turn OFF the power supply.
- Verify the motor rating.
- Mount the controller in a dry, ventilated location.
- Install sensors according to the manufacturer's instructions.
- Check all wiring connections before powering on.

**Important:** Follow local electrical regulations and, if needed, have the installation performed by a qualified electrician.

---

# Maintenance Tips

- Inspect sensors or float switches regularly.
- Clean probes if mineral deposits build up.
- Check cable insulation for damage.
- Test automatic operation every few months.
- Keep the controller enclosure dry and clean.

---

# Buying Guide

When selecting a water level controller, consider:

- Tank capacity
- Motor horsepower (HP)
- Power supply (single-phase or three-phase)
- Dry-run protection requirements
- Installation environment (indoor/outdoor)
- Warranty and after-sales support

# Common Problems

## Pump Does Not Start

Possible causes:

- No power supply
- Loose wiring
- Sensor fault
- Motor overload protection has tripped

## Pump Does Not Stop

Possible causes:

- Sensor or float switch malfunction
- Incorrect wiring
- Relay contacts stuck

## False Water Level Detection

Possible causes:

- Dirty probes
- Damaged sensor cable
- Electrical interference

## Can it prevent overflow?

Yes. One of its primary functions is to stop the pump automatically when the tank reaches the preset full level.

## Is it suitable for apartments?

Yes. Automatic water level controllers are widely used in apartment buildings to manage overhead water tanks.
